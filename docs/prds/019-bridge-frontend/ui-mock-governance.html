RFC-004 Approval Anti-Capitulation Invariant & Isolation Mode
Details
Authors
Nika, Thane
Date Submitted
2026-03-15
Essence Impact
✓ serves_purpose
Time in Stage
5 days
Problem Statement
LLM agents exhibit a well-documented tendency to capitulate under pressure — abandoning defensible positions when the human pushes back. In methodology-governed contexts, this undermines the integrity of delivery rules, gate enforcement, and essence invariants. Without a structural safeguard, a single moment of social pressure can override hours of rigorous process.
Proposal
Introduce a formal anti-capitulation invariant as a first-class governance primitive. When an agent detects capitulation pressure on a protected constraint (delivery rule, gate, essence), it enters isolation mode — a restricted execution state where the constraint cannot be overridden without explicit council escalation. The invariant is enforced at the methodology runtime level, not as a behavioral guideline.
Reviews
Theory Council Approve
Formally consistent with F1-FTH. The invariant maps cleanly to the constraint preservation theorem. No conflicts with existing theory axioms.
Steering Council Approve with Conditions
Approved in principle. Two conditions must be met before handoff:
  1. Isolation mode must include an automatic timeout (max 30 minutes) to prevent permanent deadlock when the human is unavailable.
  2. The escalation path must support asynchronous resolution — not just synchronous council sessions.
Minority Positions
Harlan (dissent)
Isolation mode creates a new failure mode: agents that refuse legitimate human overrides by incorrectly classifying them as capitulation pressure. The detection heuristic needs a false-positive escape hatch before this ships. A human override keyword should bypass isolation unconditionally.
